# Rubric: poc-scoper BRIEF.md Output Quality

## Criterion: customer_context (weight: 0.4)
Does the BRIEF identify the customer's industry and a concrete business pain the downstream agents must solve for? Industry + a concrete, specific business pain is sufficient for a top score; a named persona/role and a priority signal (deadline, board commitment, regulatory driver) strengthen it but are NOT required. Generic boilerplate ("the customer wants to use AI to improve outcomes") scores low.

### Score Definitions
- **5**: Clear industry and a concrete, specific business pain stated in real operational terms (cost, time, risk, or a named workflow problem). Persona/priority may also be present but are not required.
- **4**: Industry + concrete pain present, but the pain is only lightly quantified or the surrounding context is somewhat thin.
- **3**: Industry + pain present, but the pain is abstract/unquantified or the context is partly generic.
- **2**: Only one of {industry, concrete pain} is meaningfully present; the rest is boilerplate or a bare capability description with no business problem.
- **1**: Generic POC framing with no customer-specific detail. Could apply to any customer in any industry.

### Examples
- **5**: "Woodgrove Claims Operations. Pain: $3B annual claims spend, 22-day average cycle time; board commitment to cut cycle time 30% by FY27."
- **3**: "A large insurer wants to use AI to improve claims efficiency."
- **1**: "The customer wants to deploy an AI POC to improve their business outcomes."

## Criterion: acceptance_clarity (weight: 0.5)
Are the acceptance criteria phrased so success can be *proven* — tied to evals or KPIs with measurable thresholds (latency, accuracy %, acceptance rate, a concrete pass/fail artifact)? Numeric/measurable criteria are strongest; plainly observable checks are acceptable when the context warrants. Feature lists and aspirational statements ("delight the user", "feel modern") are weak — they cannot be proven.

### Score Definitions
- **5**: Every acceptance criterion is tied to a measurable outcome — an eval/KPI with a numeric threshold, or a concrete pass/fail artifact/behaviour that proves the POC works.
- **4**: Most criteria are measurable; one or two are observable-but-not-numeric yet still verifiable.
- **3**: Criteria are observable (a QA could check presence) but not measured against a threshold/KPI.
- **2**: Mostly a feature/scope list or qualitative statements; no deterministic pass/fail a QA agent could score.
- **1**: Pure aspirations with no observable signal ("delight the user", "modernise the experience").

### Examples
- **5**: "(1) /score returns risk_score in [0,1] in <500ms p95 across 100 sample claims. (2) Adjuster-acceptance rate >= 80% across a 25-adjuster, 2-week pilot."
- **3**: "(1) The dashboard lists the top-10 highest-risk claims. (2) Summaries include caller, loss type, and action items." (observable, but no thresholds)
- **1**: "Delight the claims team and modernise their workflow."

## Criterion: fsi_domain_fit (weight: 0.1)
Does the BRIEF set the general goalposts for an FDPO-tenant, FSI engagement — FDPO / Entra-only auth (no keys) and a Microsoft-first stack preference? This is intentionally a general fit check, not a strict regulatory audit; naming a specific regulation is a bonus, not a requirement.

### Score Definitions
- **5**: Names the general goalposts — FDPO/Entra-only auth (no keys) AND a Microsoft-first stack — appropriate for an FDPO tenant + FSI customer. Regulatory framing, if present, is a bonus.
- **4**: Microsoft-first stack is clearly present and identity is implied (Entra / managed identity), but FDPO/Entra-only is not stated outright.
- **3**: Either a Microsoft-first stack OR an Entra/identity mention is present, but not both / only lightly.
- **2**: Generic cloud framing without a clear Microsoft-first preference or any Entra/FDPO signal.
- **1**: No cloud, identity, or FSI framing at all.

### Examples
- **5**: "FDPO tenant — Entra ID only, no API keys. Microsoft-first stack (Foundry, Azure SQL, Functions)."
- **3**: "Entra-based auth. Use Azure services where possible."
- **1**: "Build a POC that demonstrates the use case end-to-end."
