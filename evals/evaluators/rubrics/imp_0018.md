# Rubric: poc-scoper BRIEF.md Output Quality

## Criterion: customer_context (weight: 0.4)
Does the BRIEF.md identify the customer's industry, top-level business pain, and decision-maker context that downstream agents need? A strong customer-context section names a specific persona (or role), states the business pain in concrete terms, and signals priority/urgency. Generic boilerplate ("the customer wants to use AI to improve outcomes") scores low.

### Score Definitions
- **5**: Explicit business pain in concrete dollars/hours/risk terms, named decision-maker persona (or role + seniority), and a clear priority signal (deadline, board commitment, regulatory driver).
- **4**: Industry + business pain + persona present, but one of the three is generic (e.g. "VP-level sponsor" without role detail) or missing a priority signal.
- **3**: Industry + pain present, but persona is vague ("the customer's team") or pain is stated abstractly without quantification.
- **2**: Only one of {industry, pain, persona} is meaningfully present; the rest is boilerplate.
- **1**: Generic POC framing with no customer-specific detail. Could apply to any customer in any industry.

### Examples
- **5**: "Woodgrove Claims Operations. Sponsor: Sarah Chen, VP Claims Automation, reporting to the COO. Pain: $3B annual claims spend, 22-day average cycle time, board commitment to cut cycle time 30% by FY27."
- **3**: "Woodgrove is a large insurance company that wants to use AI for claims. The customer's team is interested in improving efficiency."
- **1**: "The customer wants to deploy an AI POC to improve their business outcomes."

## Criterion: acceptance_clarity (weight: 0.4)
Are the acceptance criteria phrased as observable, testable outcomes that pass a "can I verify this in 30 minutes?" test? Strong acceptance criteria are deterministic checks (a metric crosses a threshold, a UI element appears, a file exists). Weak criteria are aspirational or qualitative ("delight the user", "feel modern").

### Score Definitions
- **5**: Every acceptance criterion is observable and testable in a bounded time window. Each criterion names a concrete artifact, metric, or behaviour with a pass/fail threshold.
- **4**: Most criteria are testable; one or two are slightly soft ("response feels fast") but recoverable.
- **3**: Mix of testable and aspirational criteria. Roughly half pass the 30-minute-verifiability test.
- **2**: Most criteria are aspirational or qualitative. A QA agent could not deterministically score the POC.
- **1**: Criteria are pure aspirations ("delight the user", "modernise the experience") with no observable signal.

### Examples
- **5**: "(1) `/score` endpoint returns a JSON body with `risk_score` ∈ [0,1] in < 500ms p95 across 100 sample claims. (2) Streamlit dashboard renders a table of the top-10 highest-risk claims from the last 24h."
- **3**: "(1) The model produces accurate risk scores. (2) The dashboard is easy to use."
- **1**: "Delight the claims team and modernise their workflow."

## Criterion: fsi_domain_fit (weight: 0.2)
Does the BRIEF.md reference FSI-relevant constraints that downstream agents (INFRA, DEV, ARCH) need to honour? At minimum: FDPO / Entra-only auth (no API keys), Microsoft-first stack preference, and any regulatory framing relevant to the use case (PCI-DSS, NYDFS 23, GLBA, model risk management, etc.).

### Score Definitions
- **5**: Explicit constraint section that names FDPO / Entra-only auth, Microsoft-first stack, and at least one industry-specific regulatory consideration tied to the use case.
- **4**: FDPO + Microsoft-first stack named explicitly; regulatory framing missing or generic.
- **3**: FDPO is mentioned (or implied via Entra references) but stack preference and regulatory framing are absent.
- **2**: Generic Azure-first framing without explicit FDPO or regulatory context. A non-FSI customer would not know this is an FSI POC.
- **1**: No FSI domain context at all. Could be a generic SMB POC.

### Examples
- **5**: "Constraints: FDPO tenant — Entra ID only, no API keys, no SAS tokens. Microsoft-first stack (Foundry, Azure SQL, Azure Functions). NYDFS 23 in scope: model decisions must be auditable, immutable evidence stored ≥ 7 years."
- **3**: "Auth via Entra. Use Azure services where possible."
- **1**: "Build a POC that demonstrates the use case end-to-end."
